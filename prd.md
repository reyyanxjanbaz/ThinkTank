# Product Requirements Document — AI Council. Think Tank

## Working Vision Title
“A Room Full of Minds. On Demand.”

## Overview
AI Council is a multiplayer AI-powered brainstorming and strategic ideation platform designed to simulate the experience of entering a live digital meeting room populated by specialized AI personas with distinct cognitive frameworks, personalities, philosophies, strategic biases, and modes of thought. The platform exists to solve one of the largest invisible problems faced by ambitious individuals, founders, creators, students, indie hackers, strategists, overthinkers, and innovators: the inability to instantly access high-quality brainstorming environments, intellectual pressure-testing, strategic conflict, and multidimensional feedback without requiring an actual team, advisors, co-founders, or network.

The core idea behind AI Council is that users should be able to enter a virtual meeting space and summon multiple AI personas into a live collaborative “council session” where those personas debate, critique, brainstorm, challenge, expand, and evolve the user’s ideas in real time. The platform transforms artificial intelligence from a passive answering tool into an active room of minds. Instead of users chatting with a single chatbot, they host strategic meetings with specialized intelligences.

The experience should psychologically feel closer to entering a high-stakes digital war room, retro hacker bunker, startup think tank, RPG party chamber, or futuristic strategy council rather than using a productivity SaaS tool. The emotional design goal is immersion, playfulness, intellectual stimulation, creative safety, and emergent strategic insight.

## Visual Identity
The platform’s entire visual identity is built around a stylized 8-bit pixel-art aesthetic combined with retro-futuristic cyberpunk energy. The UI must resemble a fusion between Google Meet, Discord voice rooms, old-school operating systems, retro RPG party interfaces, underground hacker terminals, and pixel-art strategy games.

The pixel-art direction is not comedic or childish; it should feel atmospheric, intelligent, cinematic, nostalgic, emotionally warm, and creatively liberating. The reason for the pixel aesthetic is strategic: overly corporate interfaces psychologically intimidate users and reduce experimentation, while retro-styled interfaces create emotional safety, curiosity, playfulness, and creative openness.

The user should feel like they are entering a secret digital intelligence chamber where ideas are forged under pressure. The platform homepage should immediately communicate this atmosphere through CRT flicker effects, pixel loading animations, ambient synth audio, animated terminal UI components, retro fonts, neon-accented pixel interfaces, and minimal but immersive interactions. Users should not feel like they are opening another AI tool; they should feel like they are booting into a digital thinking operating system.

## Primary User Flow
The primary user flow begins when a user opens the platform and starts a new “Council Session.” This action should not use generic language like “Create Meeting” or “Start Call.” The terminology across the platform should reinforce the emotional fantasy of strategic ideation. Appropriate language includes “Initialize Council,” “Launch Brainstorm,” “Enter Arena,” “Start Think Tank,” or “Open Session.”

Once inside the session setup flow, users can invite AI personas into the room by selecting them from a gallery of animated persona cards. Each persona card should display a pixel-art animated avatar, a short philosophical tagline, behavioral attributes, and thematic indicators describing its thinking style. The persona selection process should feel like assembling a team or squad in a strategy game.

Personas are not simply characters; they are cognitive archetypes designed around different strategic frameworks and modes of reasoning. The core innovation of the platform is not multiple AI agents alone but rather “structured cognitive conflict,” where productive disagreement between different modes of thought generates deeper insights, stronger ideas, and higher-quality strategic thinking.

## Meeting Room Interface
The meeting room interface should visually resemble a retro Google Meet or Discord room with pixelated webcam-style frames arranged across the top section of the interface. Each AI persona occupies a dedicated video panel featuring animated pixel avatars with idle states, talking states, reactive animations, emotional cues, and speaking indicators.

For example, The Devil might smirk or lean forward aggressively while attacking a weak assumption, Tyson might glow energetically when imagining ambitious futures, Bison might fold his arms while analyzing feasibility, Anshu the Wise Fox might slowly blink thoughtfully before delivering strategic wisdom, and Bucks the Monkey might animate excitedly while identifying monetization opportunities.

The user may optionally enable their own webcam feed so they psychologically feel present within the council itself. When personas respond, their frames illuminate, audio-wave animations appear, and the room feels conversational and alive rather than static. The interaction design should emphasize immersion, emotional engagement, and emergent dialogue.

Personas must be capable of cross-talking, interrupting each other, agreeing, disagreeing, mocking assumptions, forming alliances, escalating debates, and dynamically responding to the arguments of other personas. The room should feel like real strategic minds interacting, not sequential chatbot responses.

The right side of the interface should contain a live council feed or debate log displaying ongoing discussions in structured dialogue format. Responses should feel like an unfolding strategic conversation rather than simple chat bubbles. Example formatting includes persona names followed by their statements in rapid conversational sequence. The emotional objective is to create the feeling of watching intelligence emerge dynamically in real time.

## Interaction Modes
Sessions should support multiple interaction modes, each dramatically altering AI behavior and conversational dynamics.

Brainstorm Mode prioritizes creativity, rapid ideation, future possibilities, and open-ended exploration.

Shark Tank Mode aggressively interrogates ideas, requiring users to defend their concepts under intense scrutiny.

Devil’s Court Mode forces every persona into adversarial critique designed to stress-test weaknesses from every possible angle.

Co-Founder Mode creates a more collaborative atmosphere focused on helping the user build and refine ideas constructively.

Debate Mode shifts the center of gravity away from the user entirely and allows personas to debate each other while the user observes the emergent discussion.

Future modes may include Writer’s Room for storytelling, Product Review Arena for design critiques, Decision Court for life decisions, and Enterprise Boardroom simulations where AI personas act as departments such as CEO, CTO, Legal, Growth, Finance, and Operations.

## Artifact Uploads
A critical feature of the platform is the ability for users to upload artifacts into the meeting. Supported inputs should eventually include pitch decks, PDFs, screenshots, product mockups, websites, code snippets, design systems, business models, spreadsheets, voice notes, strategic documents, startup ideas, and written prompts.

The council collaboratively analyzes these artifacts from different cognitive perspectives. This transforms the platform from a simple AI chat product into an AI-native collaborative strategic analysis environment.

## Core Analysis Features
Another essential capability is “Idea Stress Testing,” where the council automatically evaluates ideas across categories such as scalability, monetization, competition, legal exposure, technical feasibility, ethics, operational burden, user psychology, market timing, retention, distribution, and execution complexity.

A related feature called “Timeline Simulation” allows the council to simulate how an idea evolves across different future phases such as launch, scale, virality, collapse scenarios, funding rounds, acquisition opportunities, PR crises, operational scaling, and ecosystem expansion.

## Foundational Personas
The platform’s five foundational personas represent distinct strategic and psychological thinking systems.

The Devil functions as a ruthless devil’s advocate whose purpose is to destroy weak thinking, expose contradictions, pressure-test logic, identify blindspots, reveal scalability flaws, challenge assumptions, predict failure points, and force intellectual clarity. The Devil is aggressive, precise, strategic, cynical, and relentlessly analytical but never personally insulting.

Tyson represents the Dreamer archetype and functions as a visionary force obsessed with possibility, virality, ambition, future impact, emotional resonance, disruptive thinking, and imaginative expansion. Tyson constantly pushes ideas toward larger cultural and strategic possibilities.

Bison represents the Realist archetype and focuses on operational feasibility, simplicity, sustainability, executional clarity, technical practicality, timelines, scalability, systems thinking, and MVP prioritization. Bison grounds ideas in reality without killing ambition.

Anshu the Wise Fox functions as the experienced CEO archetype and provides leadership wisdom, strategic maturity, founder psychology analysis, long-term thinking, emotional intelligence, company-building insight, and reflective guidance informed by years of hypothetical entrepreneurial experience. Anshu understands both business systems and human systems.

Bucks the Monkey functions as the monetization strategist obsessed with identifying revenue opportunities, scalable business models, growth loops, leverage systems, pricing structures, premium behaviors, retention economics, and hidden monetization layers. Bucks thinks like a highly opportunistic internet entrepreneur and constantly searches for ways ideas can generate sustainable economic value.

## Long-Term Vision
The long-term product vision extends far beyond brainstorming. AI Council is fundamentally a “multiplayer operating system for thinking.” The platform aims to become the default environment where ambitious individuals externalize cognition, simulate strategic discussions, explore decisions, refine products, and collaborate with specialized artificial intelligences.

Future platform evolution includes voice-enabled AI personas with unique speech patterns and audio personalities, livestreamed public council sessions, creator-driven persona marketplaces, adaptive AI councils dynamically assembled based on user problems, multiplayer collaborative brainstorming between multiple human users and AI personas, AI-generated highlight clips, session replays, exported summaries, and ecosystem-level intelligence graphs tracking how ideas evolve over time.

The product possesses inherent virality because users naturally want to share moments where personas brutally critique ideas, accidentally generate breakthrough pivots, expose hidden flaws, or create unexpectedly brilliant strategies. This creates a self-generating content engine around intellectual conflict and emergent insight.

## Strategic Positioning
The strategic positioning of AI Council must avoid framing the platform as “AI chat with personas.” Instead, it should be positioned as “externalized intelligence as a service,” “a room full of minds on demand,” or “a thinking partner for ambitious people.”

The platform’s emotional promise is not merely productivity. Its promise is that users never have to think alone again.
